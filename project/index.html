Cup Information

The 2026 FIFA World Cup Mexico/United States/Canada will be the 23rd edition of the FIFA World Cup
and will take place from June 11 to July 19, 2026, in Canada, the United States, and Mexico.
The triple venue was chosen on June 13, 2018, during the 68th FIFA Congress in Moscow, Russia.
This is the first time that the hosting rights have been awarded to three countries.
Mexico will be the first country to host three World Cups (after 1970 and 1986),
the United States will be the sixth to host at least two editions (after 1994), and Canada will host for the first time.

Important Dates

  • June 11, 2026 - Tournament begins
  • Thursday, June 11 - Saturday, June 27 (Group Stage fixtures)
  • Sunday, June 28 - Friday, July 3 (Round of 32 fixtures)
  • Saturday, July 4 - Tuesday, July 7 (Round of 16 fixtures)
  • Thursday, July 9 - Saturday, July 11 (Quarter-final fixtures)
  • Tuesday, July 14 and Wednesday, July 15 (Semi-final fixtures)
  • Saturday, July 18 (bronze final)
  • July 19, 2026 - Great Final (Tournament ends)
